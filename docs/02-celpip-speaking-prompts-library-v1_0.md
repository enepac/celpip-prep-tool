## Critical Notes for AI Integration

### Why these prompts are NOT scoring few-shots

The reference responses included in this library come from Paragon webinar PDFs where they are presented as *teaching examples* — not as band-calibrated samples. Adding them to the AI scorer's few-shot prompt would bias the scorer toward whatever bands the examples implicitly represent (likely Level 9-10 given that these are demo responses written by Paragon educators). Use the **scored samples in Section 12 of the rubric reference** for scoring calibration; use prompts in this library for **practice prompt presentation only**.

### What this library provides

- 8+ official Paragon practice prompts spanning all 8 task types
- For Tasks where Paragon-published reference responses exist, the response is included with explicit "TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED" tagging
- Image-based tasks (3, 4, 5, 8) include text descriptions of the image content the original prompt referenced — the AI/product can either generate equivalent images or use these descriptions as text-only fallbacks
- Each prompt is tagged with task type, prep time, speaking time, and source

### Recommended in-product use

- Each prompt becomes one practice exercise in the v1 task library
- 8+ prompts is enough for a launchable v1; more can be authored over time
- Reference responses become the "Show me what a strong response looks like" reveal AFTER the user records their own attempt
- Prompts should rotate so users don't memorize a single prompt per task type

## Task 1: Giving Advice

**Format:** 30 sec prep | 90 sec speaking | Speak directly to the person | Provide 3+ suggestions with reasons | Friendly, helpful tone | Closing with encouragement

### Prompt 1.1 — First Day at New Software Job
*Source: Scoring and Performance Standards webinar*

> Your friend was hired for a position at a software development company. Since she only recently graduated from university, this will be the first job she has ever had. Give her advice on how she should prepare for her first day at work.

**Prep notes (from webinar):**
- Try to make a good first impression
- Dress up
- Introduce yourself
- Remember people's names

**Reference response (TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED):**
The webinar shows a partial outline only. Suggest using the closing pattern: *"Well, I hope that helps you. Good luck on your first day!"*

## Task 2: Talking about a Personal Experience

**Format:** 30 sec prep | 60 sec speaking | Use past tense | Address all sub-tasks (usually 2-3 questions) | Include descriptive details | Communicate how you felt

### Prompt 2.1 — Acted as a Leader
*Source: Scoring and Performance Standards webinar*

> Talk about a time when you acted as a leader. Maybe you can talk about taking on a leadership role in a family, school, or company activity. What was the activity, how did you lead others, and what was the final result?

**Sub-tasks:** What was the activity? + How did you lead others? + What was the final result? (all three required)

**Reference response (TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED):**

> "I am going to talk about a time when, as a teacher, I took some international students on a tour of Vancouver. About 12 students came out that day. They were from all over the world and none of them knew Vancouver very well.
>
> In the morning, we met at the Steam Clock, which is a famous historical landmark in Vancouver. Then we went to Chinatown — it's one of the largest in North America. After that, I took them to Stanley Park, so that they could enjoy the city's natural side. Finally, I took them to Robson Street, so that they could do some shopping for souvenirs and brand name goods.
>
> By the end of the day, the students had a pretty good feel for the city and they had a great time. Several of them said that they were surprised by all that Vancouver has to offer."

## Task 3: Describing a Scene

**Format:** 30 sec prep | 60 sec speaking | Present continuous + present simple tense | General statement → details → connections | Use spatial vocabulary

### Prompt 3.1 — Busy Post Office
*Source: Scoring and Performance Standards webinar*
*Image description:* A busy post office. People of all ages. Counter staff helping customers. A man in a green sleeveless T-shirt holding parcels at the counter. A boy on the far left holding money. A woman with long hair and a backpack waiting in line. Several other customers in queues separated by blue rope barriers. Boxes and packages on shelves behind the counter.

> Describe the scene in this picture. The person you are speaking to cannot see what you are looking at.

**Reference response excerpts (TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED):**
- General statement: *"This is a busy post office. There are people of all ages in the post office."*

## Task 5: Comparing and Persuading

**Format:** 60 sec Part 1 prep (no speaking) | 60 sec Part 2 prep | 60 sec Part 2 speaking | Choose one option → persuade against new option

### Prompt 5.1 — Mountain Town Accommodation
*Source: Scoring and Performance Standards webinar*

**Part 1 (silent prep):**

> You and your hiking team need to choose where to stay overnight in a small mountain town before your next trip. You find two suitable options. Using the pictures and information below, choose the option that you prefer. In the next part, you will need to persuade the hiking group organizer that your choice is the better choice.

*Option A — Hostel:* $17/night, 6 people per room, shared bathrooms, shared kitchen and living room, no food included.

*Option B — Bed and Breakfast:* $120/night, private bedrooms and bathrooms, small kitchen in your suite, includes hot breakfast of your choice.

**Part 2 (speaking):**

> Your hiking group organizer is suggesting another accommodation. Persuade her that the accommodation you chose is more suitable by comparing the two.

*Organizer's choice — Rent a Camper:* $235/night, beds for 4 people, portable toilet, small kitchen and living room, seaside view.

**Prep grid (Main Points of Comparison) for if user chose B&B:**

| Feature | Camper (organizer) | B&B (your choice) |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | $235 | $120 → cheaper |
| Bedrooms | shared | private, more comfortable |
| Bathrooms | portable toilet | private, more convenient |
| Kitchen/breakfast | small kitchen, no food | small kitchen + breakfast included |

**Reference outline (TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED):**

> "I think the bed and breakfast is a much better choice for us. First of all, when we look at cost versus value… The sleeping arrangement is much more comfortable at the B and B. We also need to consider the toilet situation. The B and B also offers the convenience of a hot breakfast."

## Task 6: Dealing with a Difficult Situation

**Format:** 60 sec prep | 60 sec speaking | Choose ONE option (Either Person A or Person B) | Address that person directly | Considerate but firm tone

### Prompt 6.1 — Office Kitchen Water Leak
*Source: Scoring and Performance Standards webinar*

> You are the office supervisor and you notice that water has leaked all over the kitchen floor. It needs to be cleaned right away, but Sasha, the employee responsible for this, is just leaving to meet a friend for lunch, and she doesn't want to cancel it. She asks her co-worker, David, to clean the floor instead, but David refuses.
>
> **Choose ONE:**
> EITHER explain to Sasha why she should change her lunch time, cancel lunch with her friend, and clean up the floor.
> OR explain to David why he should clean up the floor for Sasha.

**Prep outline (TEACHING EXAMPLE if user picked David — NOT BAND-LABELED):**
1. Sasha has plans — shame for her to delay or cancel them
2. Teamwork! We need to help each other out sometimes
3. Probably won't take too long

**Softening phrases:**
- "I hope that you can understand that…"
- "I think we can all agree that…"
- "Let's try to look at it this way…"

## Task 8: Describing an Unusual Situation

**Format:** 30 sec prep | 60 sec speaking | TWO sub-tasks: describe + ask/invite | Phone-call frame | Match tone to scenario (excited if interesting)

### Prompt 8.1 — Hot Dog Mascot at Soccer Game
*Source: Speaking_Test_8.pdf think-aloud*

*Image description:* University soccer game. A person dressed in a hot dog mascot costume on a soccer field. Wearing a green wig (looks like relish), red hat with hot dog topping, sunglasses, red sleeve cover, black shorts. The letter "W" on the costume. Stadium signage reads "UNIVER… GAMES." Crowd visible in background.

> You are at a university soccer game and see a strange character. You decide to call your father. Describe what you see and ask him if he wants to come see it.

**5W prep grid (TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED):**

| Who | What | Where | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Uncle, excellent soccer player | Half-time show, mascots playing a game, charity | Soccer stadium, university, Western | Volunteering his time, representing his university |

**Reference response (TEACHING EXAMPLE — NOT BAND-LABELED):**

> "Hi Dad! I'm at the soccer game and I just had to call you because there's something quite interesting happening here.
>
> It's half-time and they brought out two teams to play an exhibition match. But they aren't normal teams — all the players are dressed up in silly costumes.
>
> I was watching the mascots play and you'll never guess who I saw — your brother! He's wearing a hilarious hot dog costume and a green wig, which I think is supposed to be relish. He also has the letter 'W' on his costume, which I think represents the university you both attended, Western University, right?
>
> As you know, he's an excellent soccer player. Even in this silly costume, he's still dominating the play. He's already scored two goals!
>
> I screamed his name and he smiled, but I don't think he recognized me. If you were here, I'm sure he would notice us.
>
> I think you should come down here and cheer him on — he'll be so happy to see you. And it's all for charity — I think some of the money will go towards your university."
